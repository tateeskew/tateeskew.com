## First Ten Standouts

* Alan Licht – Havens [listen/purchase][1]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>I'm not sure what else needs to be said about Alan and his guitar music. This new album explores repetition and subtle morphing pieces that will fill you with goodwill.</em></span>
* I4A – I4A 3 [listen/purchase][2]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>I4A continues to fly under the radar making "dishwashing music" that shows an immense amount of maturity through smart composition and ideas that are modern sharp takes with Traffic and Kraut origins, but somehow wholly different.</em></span>
* Läuten der Seele – Die Reise zur Monsalwäsche [listen/purchase][3]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">This is the final installment of  Christian Schoppik's "Water" trilogy. The first two showing up on this yearly list in the past as well. He makes beautiful collages that don't get long in the tooth or introduce boredom. </span>_
* Wussy – Cincinatti Ohio [listen/purchase][4]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">As a fellow Midwesterner, this record is almost achingly too on the nose. Wussy continues to write music that immediately shows that they've listened deeply to thousands of records and they are able to relay their existence through your speakers.</span>_
* Nala Sinephro – Endlessness [listen/purchase][5]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">This is what happens when you have an ear for a journey and surround yourself with great musicians. If Broadcast wanted to make a record with mid-era Miles, you'd find something like this on the tape.</span>_
* Jeff Parker ETA IVtet – The Way Out of Easy [listen/purchase][6]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>An album that has an immediate impact on first listen and still gives upon repeated listening. Which is exactly what Jeff, Anna, Jay, and Josh are doing in the room together…listening.</em></span>
* METZ – Up On Gravity Hill [listen/purchase][7]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>For me, this is METZ finally delivering the melody that I knew they were capable of, without losing the raw power that they somehow deliver in trio form. This will pummel you from start to finish…and you'll immediately replay it in its entirety. </em></span>
* Mount Eerie – Night Palace [listen/purchase][8]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">I think perhaps Phil has finally made an album that is the essence of what he truly is as an artist/human. It's all here, everything he's embodied over the last decades. Introspective, quaint, fuzzy, cloudy, and abrupt. </span>_
* Blood Incantation – Absolute Elsewhere [listen/purchase][9]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">This is the best death metal band going and this is the best record they've made to date. Obviously they've been deep diving into the German ambient crates and it has imprinted itself into their approach. This is gigantic and sprawling. Take your time.</span>_
* Merope – V?jula [listen/purchase][10]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">This is a beautiful album of recordings rooted in Lithuanian folk music, but tied together through the ancient and modernity. It's perfectly paced and the emotions communicated are easily received. As artists, we only hope we can fully realize our ideas like this.</span>_

—

* Tara Jane O'Neil – The Cool Cloud of Okayness [listen/purchase][11]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">TJO continues to make music that tickles her fancy at any given moment. She's careful in her craft and as she shifts styles, she always has a common thread throughout all of her albums…she's got immense taste.</span>_
* Klimperei – Cirque Klimperei [listen/purchase][12]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>This is a collection of pieces from the history of recorded music from Klimperei and it's so perfectly well done. Kudos to Time Released Sound & Time Sensitive Materials for doing such a wonderful job sequencing and packaging this up. A must listen AND physical purchase.</em></span>
* Eric Fourman – Dread [listen/purchase][13]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Eric Fourman has been inconspicuously making music in the Middle TN area for years. Quietly releasing some of the best drone music pieces without any speck of pretension. Not merely fucking around, he obviously develops compositions that first and foremost are for himself.</em></span>
* The Hard Quartet – The Hard Quartet [listen/purchase][14]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Rio's Song may be one of the coolest sing-along simple tunes released in quite some time. Jim White's drumming is so perfectly good here while the contributions from Matt, Emmett, and Stephen don't feel like throwaways you get with most "supergroups".</span>_
* Gillian Welch and David Rawlings – Woodland [listen/purchase][15]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Do I really have to put something here? Empty Trainload Of Sky sounds like it was written 50 years ago and is already a classic. The rest is just the best Nashville duo of the last 25+ years. Enough said.</span>_
* German Error Message – German Error Message (S/T) [listen/purchase][16]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Sticking to the Middle TN area of my home, German Error Message has continuously released music that combines experimental folk with the bedroom recordings most musicians make for only themselves. You can tell he's a lifer and he's waiting on nobody to put things into the world.</em></span>
* Melos Kalpa – Melos Kalpa (S/T) [listen/purchase][17]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>I wouldn't necessarily call this a supergroup, but perhaps for me it is. This first album from a collection of musicians that I admire, showcases how a group of people improvising and piecing together ideas can result in a wonderfully moving abstraction of feelings and ears.</em></span>
* Amblare – Amblare (S/T) [listen/purchase][18]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>You are only allowed to listen to this album via large amounts of volume. You know immediately that this is Midwestern in origin: big drums and guitars, playing with time sigs, melodic vocalizations and lyrics revolving around heavy emotions.</em></span>
* Pan American & Kramer – Reverberations Of Non-Stop Traffic On Redding Road [listen/purchase][19]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Mark Nelson teams up with Kramer to create almost exactly what you would expect from these two collaborating. This is super patient stuff that resembles the sun shining off of the lake as I currently look out the window.</em></span>
* daily rituals – all the ways in which i am mute [listen/purchase][20]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Incredibly wonderful field recording collage work that lets you take part in the disintegration of time with microsound and elecroacoustic approaches.</em></span>
* Klaus Weise – Sabiha Sabiya [listen/purchase][21]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Klaus Weise from Popol Vuh gets a reissue of this past tape that was originally released in 1982 on Aquamarin Verlag, the distinguished publisher of New Age books and music and it feels as full of place as the music he used to make with his aforementioned collective. </em></span>
* Myriam Gendron – Mayday [listen/purchase][22]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Look, I could use comparison of Myriam to Nico, but that would cheapen what she really does…but would it?</em></span>
* Los Days – Dusty Dreams [listen/purchase][23]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>I have immense respect for Tommy Guerrero. I think Tommy and Max Schaaf are the epitome of how skateboarding creativity radiates out into other life works. This is the newest sun-drenched exploration of sound with his friend Josh Lippi and it delivers as per usual.</em></span>
* Belong – Realistic IX [listen/purchase][24]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">I wasn't sure if we'd ever get another Belong record, but I'm sure glad we got one. This one finds them drowning in wash that isn't subtle by any means, but it's all the better because it's cloudy with a chance of mist all day. Headphones on for this one.</span>_
* Arcwelder – Continue [listen/purchase][25]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>There was a reason we'd pile into a warm bar in the Midwestern winter with piss soaked bathroom floors…Arcwelder were playing.</em></span>
* Magic Tuber Stringband – Needlefall [listen/purchase][26]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">This is a beautifully put together recording of an evolving palette of string music. Improvisation through loosely based structure is what it sounds like to me. Exploration of sounds through togetherness and a shared record collection. Outstanding stuff.</span>_
* Itasca – Imitation of War [listen/purchase][27]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Sometimes Itasca comes across as a sister artist to The Weather Station who was recorded in a studio that understands that the process is just as important as the end result.</span>_
* Derek Monypeny – The Oppositional Imagination [listen/purchase][28]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Derek makes music here that differs in style from track to track, but all encompassing what I love about making my own guitar music…whether prepared, droned, strummed, or aggressively picked, you'll find emotion hurled into the room.</span>_
* Ned Collette – Our Other History [listen/purchase][29]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Ned returned this year with this particular album that recalls his absolutely amazing 2018 album, Old Chestnut. This music is serious business, and you know it. </span>_
* Seabuckthorn – this warm, this late [listen/purchase][30]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Seabuckthorn is a cold hardy plant that puts on thousands of small berries that are found to be medicinal. So are these musical pieces found on the newest collections of sounds from Andy as he crafts beautiful improvisations with friends in tow.</em></span>
* Finom – Not God [listen/purchase][31]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Dare I say this might be the best thing that Tweedy has produced…ever? These women understand what they want to say and how to say it. Melodic, witty, and perfectly arranged. Kudos to Tom Schick for such a fantastic sounding recording. **Chef's Kiss**</em></span>
* Stefan Christensen – In Time [listen/purchase][32]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">You can definitely hear the Träd Gräs Och Stenar family tree here. At times Stefan and crew will veer into kraut territory, but firmly holding onto their obvious psych tendencies. Fire up the Marantz and throw it on already.</span>_
* Brandon Tani – The Road Was Bent From The Way We Took It [listen/purchase][33]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Tani takes you around to the places that make you wonder if you could recreate daily in your life if given nothing but some love and time. This is supposed to be abstract music, but it feels like it has purpose. Found sound and concrète approach, but with focused ideas.</span>_
* Winged Wheel – Big Hotel [listen/purchase][34]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Exploring more of the motorik sounds and drowning the vocal in a room the size of Central Station. 12XU will never let you down, kids.</span>_
* Mystery Waitress – Bright Black Night [listen/purchase][35]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Sometimes I want to hear something fairly pop influenced, but somehow it still holds on to threads of being pretty without all of the preciousness. That seems to be a theme that falls out of New Zealand quite often (think Tiny Ruins). Flying Nun seems to have had a knack for finding this stuff for 40 plus years and here's the latest in that long line. Mountain, what a song.</em></span>
