There were a lot of amazing albums released this year. As per usual, I've spent countless hours engulfed in new releases along with the albums of the past that I love. I couldn't possibly list everything I listened to this year that I thought was worth sharing, but I'm going to list thirty-five albums here that really connected with me this year. I listened to all of these albums a lot this year and these were the ones that I was often returning to in times of not deep diving into back catalogs. These are in no particular order, but I will say that the first ten albums I have listed here are real standouts for me this year!

If you enjoy these albums, please spend some of your hard earned money on the ones that you really connect with. Every bit helps these artists continue to make compelling records. I'm also posting some of the album covers from this collection of records to showcase some of the artwork.

### First Ten Standouts

* Joe Rainey – Niineta : [listen/purchase][1]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Envelope pushing Native American record engulfed in drum/singing…skin-tingling singing</em></span>
* Eric Chenaux – Say Laura : [listen/purchase][2]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Incredibly smart jazz informed experiments with voice and guitar</span>_
* Jeannine Schulz – Humble : [listen/purchase][3]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Ambient micro-shifts of drift full of organic movements</span>_
* Läuten der Seele – Läuten der Seele [listen/purchase][4]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Mangled collages in the arena of Mr. Rogers</em></span>
* John Scofield – John Scofield : [listen/purchase][5]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>World class solo guitar of original pieces and interpretations of classics</em></span>
* Art Of Primitive Sound (W. Maioli, P. Meyer, L. Maioli) – Strumenti Musicali Della Preistoria: Il Paleolitico : [listen/purchase][6]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Primitive instruments in wide open recording spaces</em></span>
* Mali Obomsawin – Sweet Tooth : [listen/purchase][7]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Native song/singing framed within beautiful jazz freak-outs</em></span>
* John Also Bennett – Out there in the middle of nowhere : [listen/purchase][8]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Patient lap steel guitar and field recordings in desolate landscapes</em></span>
* Robert Haigh – Human Remains : [listen/purchase][9]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Harold Budd inspired works of piano and space</em></span>
* Peter Phippen – Into the Ancient : [listen/purchase][10]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>World renowned ancient flute player with shimmering surroundings</em></span>

—

* Horse Lords – Comradely Objects : [listen/purchase][11]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Knitting Factory ready wicked smart mathy push and pull experiments in rock</em></span>
* Firebreather – Dwell In The Fog : [listen/purchase][12]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Super heavy and melodic Pabst induced Gibson LP doom sludge</em></span>
* Lifeguard – Crowd Can Talk : [listen/purchase][13]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Young kids channeling Unwound and Southern Records classics</em></span>
* Mike Baggetta / Jim Keltner / Mike Watt – Everywhen We Go : [listen/purchase][14]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Improvised stomps with jazz leanings trio work</em></span>
* ' t Geruis – Bain D’Étoiles : [listen/purchase][15]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Micro music that pushes repetition in the best way possible</em></span>
* Jon Camp – Jon Camp : [listen/purchase][16]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Guitar rag freak folk journey</em></span>
* The Web of Lies – Nude With Demon : [listen/purchase][17]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Heady scuzz that channels everything from Sonic Youth to Satisfact in garage lore</em></span>
* Cafe Kaput – Maritime (Themes and Textures) : [listen/purchase][18]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Channeling the best of what Eno had to offer in his early ambient inventions. Top shelf</em></span>
* Rosales – Woven Songs : [listen/purchase][19]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Beautiful slow shifting drone</em></span>
* Thought Forms – Clean : [listen/purchase][20]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Psych guitar based music without all of the tropes and reimagined acoustically</em></span>
* Friendship – Love the Stranger : [listen/purchase][21]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Songwriter based tunes channeling Richard Thompson and David Berman</em></span>
* Naujawanan Baidar – Khedmat Be Khalq : [listen/purchase][22]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Afghan street psych pumps that you can dance to</em></span>
* Lay Llamas – Goud : [listen/purchase][23]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Experiments in pop song writing but channeling krautrock, acid-folk, and Foetus</em></span>
* Adam H. – Floods : [listen/purchase][24]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Outstanding shifting long-form quiet to loud ambient passages</em></span>
* Yellow6 – A Change In the Weather : [listen/purchase][25]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Perfectly paced guitar based music for those that love Labradford, Pan American and Alan's guitar work in Low</em></span>
* Deniz Cuylan : Rings of Jupiter : [listen/purchase][26]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Super mature guitar pieces channeling the likes of The Durutti Column</em></span>
* Éliane Radigue & Frédéric Blondy – Occam XXV : [listen/purchase][27]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Long form organ drone that changes with every listen</em></span>
* Gary Peters / Talk West – The Different Same : [listen/purchase][28]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Pedal steel with just a bit more structure than the great Susan Alcorn</em></span>
* Desolate Shrine – Fires of the Dying World : [listen/purchase][29]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Swirling black metal that you are constantly catching up with</em></span>
* Golden Feelings – Golden Feelings : [listen/purchase][30]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Sun saturated reverb drift with a hint of Pieter Nooten or Robin Guthrie</em></span>
* Beaunoise – Buchlaworks: Module III : [listen/purchase][31]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Smart improvised modular explorations</em></span>
* Aldous Harding – Warm Chris : [listen/purchase][32]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Songwriter-based music with unique phrasing wrapped perfectly within John Parish approaches</em></span>
* Sawako – Stella Epoca : [listen/purchase][33]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Falling apart fragments of found sound, horns, piano/synth composed framing Sawako's voice with perfection</em></span>
* Pan American – The Patience Fader : [listen/purchase][34]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Stillness defined through delays, guitar, attention to detail…and patience</em></span>
* Undeath – It's Time To Rise From the Grave : [listen/purchase][35]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Killer classic death metal songs not over-produced like so many in the genre these days</em></span>
