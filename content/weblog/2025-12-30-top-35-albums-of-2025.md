<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Lucy Gooch – Desert Window</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://lucygooch.bandcamp.com/album/desert-window" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<strong>  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Desert Window</em></span></strong><span style="color: #808080;"><em> sits somewhere between folk song and dream logic. Lucy Gooch’s layered vocals and soft electronics recall mid-era <strong>Björk</strong> and <strong>Kate Bush</strong> at their most exploratory, but scaled way down and turned inward. It’s quiet, patient music that opens up the longer you stay with it. Maybe my favorite album of the year.</em></span>
</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Ron Heglin – Tom Djll – Duos for Voice and Runglers</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://publiceyesore.bandcamp.com/album/duos-for-voice-and-runglers" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>A standout for me this year, <strong>Duos for Voice and Runglers</strong> feels like two signals meeting in fog, neither fully translating the other. <strong>Ron Heglin</strong>’s voice bends and mutates like a half-remembered language while <strong>Tom Djll</strong>’s runglers spark, stutter, and short-circuit around it. The result is a fragile truce between flesh and machine, hovering just long enough to leave a trace.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Friendship – Caveman Wakes Up</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://friendshipphl.bandcamp.com/album/caveman-wakes-up" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Caveman Wakes Up</strong> feels like sunlight hitting stone for the first time, followed by a sideways grin. <strong>Friendship</strong> writes songs that wander with intent, full of sharp observation, dry humor, and melodies that feel casually worn but carefully placed. It’s deeply smart music that pretends not to be, unfolding at its own unhurried pace.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Daniel Knox – Mercado 48</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://danielknox.bandcamp.com/album/mercado-48" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Mercado 48</strong> unfolds in hushed piano figures and a voice that sounds weary but steady. <strong>Daniel Knox</strong> writes with a rare clarity about isolation, doubt, and endurance, touching the human condition without grand statements or sentimentality. The restraint gives the songs their weight and staying power.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>The Bats – Corner Coming Up</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://thebats.bandcamp.com/album/corner-coming-up" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Corner Coming Up</strong> lands as another quiet affirmation from a band that has always trusted small gestures. <strong>The Bats</strong> sound completely at ease here, writing songs that feel modest on the surface but deeply assured underneath. For longtime listeners, it’s comforting without being nostalgic, proof that their instincts are still intact.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>James McMurtry – The Black Dog and The Wandering Boy</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://jamesmcmurtry.bandcamp.com/album/the-black-dog-and-the-wandering-boy" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>The Black Dog and the Wandering Boy</strong> shows <strong>James McMurtry</strong> operating at full strength, sharp, unsentimental, and quietly funny in the way only truly smart writing can be. “<strong>Sons of the Second Sons</strong>” stands as one of the year’s best songs and one of the finest he has written, cutting deep while keeping its balance. The record carries that same mix of wit, moral clarity, and hard-earned perspective throughout.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Mike Majikowski – Invisible</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://mikemajkowski.bandcamp.com/album/invisible" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Invisible</strong> centers on double bass, but it breathes and drifts like the best ambient music. <strong>Mike Majkowski</strong> lets tones emerge, recede, and blur at the edges, turning resonance and silence into equal partners. It’s deeply physical music that listens as much as it speaks, slow-moving and quietly absorbing.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Sean McCann – The Leopard</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://recitalprogram.bandcamp.com/album/the-leopard" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>The Leopard</strong> is a meticulously constructed opera that dissolves narrative into sound, voice, and hallucinated detail. <strong>Sean McCann</strong> moves between radio play, ritual, and dream logic, letting scenes eat themselves and reform in slow, uncanny cycles. It’s immersive and disorienting work that rewards surrender more than interpretation.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Greet Death – Die In Love</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://greetdeath.bandcamp.com/album/die-in-love" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><strong>Die In Love</strong> finds <strong>Greet Death</strong> settling into their weight, writing with more patience and intention than before. The songs feel sturdier and more self-aware, as if the band has learned how to stand inside the noise rather than push against it. Even so, the record suggests a horizon just beyond reach, making their growth feel both earned and unfinished.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Little Mazarn – Mustang Island</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://littlemazarn.bandcamp.com/album/mustang-island" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Mustang Island</strong> moves with a restless curiosity, its songs leaning into landscape and memory without ever settling into easy answers. <strong>Little Mazarn</strong> blends warm acoustic elements with unusual rhythmic and sonic shifts, creating music that feels lived-in and thoughtful. There’s a sense of wandering here, like watching light change over a place you think you know and realizing you only just arrived.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>John Camp – Proceed</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://joncamp.bandcamp.com/album/proceed" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Proceed</strong> is precise and unpretentious, built from clean lines and patient movement. <strong>John Camp</strong> shapes sound with purpose, letting patterns emerge and shift without excess. His guitar playing is great as per usual and the result is music that feels both thoughtful and deliberate, rewarding close attention and lingering in the quiet between notes.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Aiko Takahashi – The Grass Harp</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://laaps.bandcamp.com/album/the-grass-harp" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Small piano figures surface and disappear, never insisting on themselves. What <strong>Aiko Takahashi</strong> builds here is less about melody than attention, a slow tuning of the ear to touch, breath, and residue. <strong>The Grass Harp</strong> rewards patience by quietly changing how you listen, even after it ends.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Sally Anne Morgan – Second Circle The Horizon</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://sallyannemorgan.bandcamp.com/album/second-circle-the-horizon" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;">Circling rather than advancing, the music moves like a body finding its own pace again. <strong>Sally Anne Morgan</strong> lets fiddle, banjo, and subtle textures trace slow arcs that feel shaped by weather, breath, and repetition. <strong>Second Circle The Horizon</strong> listens closely to cycles rather than moments, rewarding attention to how things return slightly changed.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Chrome Chasm – The Hermit</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://chromechasm.bandcamp.com/album/the-hermit" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;">Long, patient passages stretch out and slowly shift, built from guitars, pedal steel, and soft brass that bleed into each other. <strong>Chrome Chasm</strong> favors atmosphere over momentum, letting pieces change almost imperceptibly. <strong>The Hermit</strong> works best when you stop looking for direction and let the sound settle in around you as if you were listening to the best of Philip Glass.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Mekons – Horror</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://mekons.bandcamp.com/album/horror" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><strong>Horror</strong> finds the long-running <strong>The Mekons</strong> doing what they’ve always done best: taking serious ideas and letting them breathe through rough-edged, restless music. Lyrics jump from historical reckoning to present-day malaise, and multiple voices shift the focus like a conversation that won’t settle for easy answers. The result is an album that’s both pointed and surprisingly warm, full of sharp critique and the band’s lived-in sense of collective strength, and it underlines why they’re still vital after all these decades.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Tropical Fuck Storm – Fairyland Codex</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://tropicalfstorm.bandcamp.com/album/fairyland-codex" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><strong>Fairyland Codex</strong> feels like a controlled delirium, raw edges and strange turns unfolding with brutal clarity. <strong>Tropical Fuck Storm</strong> blends noise, melody, satire, and bleak humor in ways that never feel accidental or sloppy. For a band that seems capable of anything, this record pushes their restless inventiveness into sharper focus without losing the off-kilter charm that makes them so amazing. They can do no wrong in my book.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Schatterau – Übers Jahr</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://schatterau.bandcamp.com/album/bers-jahr" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Quiet, seasonal, and attentive, this record moves at a human pace. <strong>Schatterau</strong> works with field recordings, piano, and soft electronics in a way that feels observational rather than expressive, documenting time passing rather than dramatizing it. <strong>Übers Jahr</strong> rewards repeated listening, not through revelation, but through familiarity slowly deepening.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Pale Blue Eyes – New Place</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://paleblueeyesmusic.bandcamp.com/album/new-place" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;">Built with their own hands and clearly trusted, the songs settle in without strain. <strong>Pale Blue Eyes</strong> let guitars, synths, and steady rhythms lock together naturally, nothing overthought or undercut. <strong>New Place</strong> proves how rare it is to self-record and still sound this sure of yourself.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Chris Brokaw – Ghost Ship</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://chrisbrokaw12xu.bandcamp.com/album/ghost-ship" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Keeping up with my friend <strong>Chris Brokaw</strong> almost feels beside the point. He writes like someone taking notes while moving through the world, attentive and unsentimental. <strong>Ghost Ship</strong> drifts between memory and presence, songs arriving worn but intact, carrying the quiet authority of someone who has lived inside the questions long enough to stop explaining them.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Shiner – Believemeyou</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://shinerkc.bandcamp.com/album/believeyoume" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;">The songs hit like well-placed turns in a familiar road, nothing wasted and nothing rushed. <strong>Shiner</strong> locks smart melodies into tightly wound structures, letting tension and release do the talking, just as they've done for decades now. <strong>Believeyoume</strong> sounds seasoned rather than softened, proof that precision and feeling do not cancel each other out.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Thor & Friends – Heathen Spirituals</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://thorharris.bandcamp.com/album/heathen-spirituals" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Heathen Spirituals</strong> unfolds as three long instrumental movements that feel meditative yet charged, like ritual music pulled from a minimalist dream. The ensemble builds from simple marimba lines into layered sound worlds where cello drones, winds, and even a wordless choir give weight to every shift. Recorded live in an empty auditorium, the music moves slowly but insistently, a kind of spacious, hymn-like ambient work that suggests both quiet reflection and something approaching awe.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Lathe of Heaven – Aurora</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://latheofheaven.bandcamp.com/album/aurora" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>This one hit me right away and stayed there because of the guitar lines and vocal melodies that many Flock of Seagulls fans will love. <strong>Lathe of Heaven</strong> has a real feel for mood and melody here, songs that glow and fray at the edges without losing their center. <strong>Aurora</strong> already feels strong and considered, and it also makes me excited to hear what happens when they tighten the screws just a bit more next time.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Ben McElroy – Elkwort</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://laaps.bandcamp.com/album/elkwort" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><strong>Elkwort</strong> feels less like a composed record and more like something overheard. Breath, bow scrape, and wheezing organ drift in and out, never settling long enough to become comfortable. There is a rural, almost medicinal quality to it, as if these sounds were gathered rather than written. It rewards patience, not with big moments, but with a sense of being quietly altered by the time it ends.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Eli Winter – A Trick Of The Light</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://eliwinter.bandcamp.com/album/a-trick-of-the-light" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>This record is <strong>Eli Winter</strong> stepping fully into himself. The playing is open and generous, curious without showing off, and confident enough to let ideas breathe. It moves easily between folk, jazz, and abstraction, but always sounds grounded and human.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>The Necks – Disquiet</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://thenecksau.bandcamp.com/album/disquiet" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><strong>Disquiet</strong> tests your patience and rewards it in equal measure. This is the record where <strong>The Necks</strong> unapologetically take up more than three hours of your life with slowly unfolding, improvisational terrain that feels alive and weathered at the same time. It’s hypnotic without being easy, mysterious without being opaque, and when it clicks it feels like you are breathing the same air as these three.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Jogging House – Kiosk</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://jogginghouse.bandcamp.com/album/kiosk" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>Kiosk</strong> is <strong>Jogging House</strong> in a slightly sunnier mood, where loopy tape fragments and hazy melodies feel personal and gentle rather than shadowy. The whole thing was built by chopping up tape loops and running them through a sampler and modular gear, then recording straight to tape in live takes, which gives it an unguarded, human energy.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>The Ex – If Your Mirror Breaks</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://theex.bandcamp.com/album/if-your-mirror-breaks" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>This one feels less like confrontation and more like communion. <strong>The Ex</strong> still clang and surge, but there’s a warmth under the abrasion, a sense of people listening hard to each other while the world rattles outside. The songs feel handmade and a little crooked, full of motion, humor, and unease. Strange music that somehow leaves you steadier than when you started.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Water Damage – Live At Le Guess Who?</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://waterdamage12xu.bandcamp.com/album/live-at-le-guess-who" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>This record is less like a performance and more like a condition you submit to. <strong>Water Damage</strong> lock into a single idea and refuse to blink, pushing repetition until it turns viscous and hallucinatory. Time stretches, details warp, and what starts as brute force slowly becomes meditative. Music as pressure system. Simple in the best way, smart in how long it holds.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Old Saw – The Wringing Cloth</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://lobbyartrecs.bandcamp.com/album/the-wringing-cloth" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;">The recording here from <strong>Old Saw</strong> feels quietly unsettled, like folk music remembering something it would rather not. <strong>Old Saw</strong> stretch familiar acoustic shapes until they thin out and blur, leaving space where certainty used to live. Nothing here rushes, nothing insists. It just hangs in the air, damp and unresolved, and somehow feels truer for it.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Michael Grigoni * Pan•American – New World, Lonely Ride</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://panamerican.bandcamp.com/album/new-world-lonely-ride" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;"><strong>New World, Lonely Ride</strong> is a road trip at sunrise with no destination, just the hum of asphalt and the slow bloom of atmosphere around you. <strong>Michael Grigoni</strong> and <strong>Pan•American</strong> weave distant electronics, sparse guitar, and field recordings into something both expansive and intimate. It’s not beat-driven. It’s mood-driven, a study in space where silence counts as much as sound. A lonely ride that somehow feels like company when you need it.</span></em></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Ancient Death – Ego Dissolution</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://ancient-death.bandcamp.com/album/ego-dissolution" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Not a lot of metal on the list this year, but <strong>Ego Dissolution</strong> is a stand-out. The riffs and rhythms are steeped in tradition yet feel alive and unpredictable, like old school mechanics running through an cosmic filter. It's an album that sounds like it’s wrestling with its own shadow and winning.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Piers Faccini & Ballaké Sissoko – When The Word Was Song</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://piersfacciniballaksissoko.bandcamp.com/album/ep-when-the-word-was-song" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>This recording exemplifies music from before music was separated into styles. Voice, guitar, and kora move with an easy gravity, each note chosen, nothing wasted. It’s intimate without being fragile, ancient without feeling precious. Songs as shared memory, passed hand to hand, still warm when you receive them.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Cheer-Accident – Admission</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://cheeraccident.bandcamp.com/album/admission" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><strong>Admission</strong> feels like a band that never lost the joy of surprise. <strong>Cheer-Accident</strong> twist and pivot with gleeful unpredictability, leaping from jagged art-rock hooks to jazz-tinged turns and oddball harmonies that land just when you think you know the road they’re on. It’s smart without being precious, strange without being alienating, and full of moments that make you both grin and scratch your head. An album that rewards curiosity and refuses to sit still. They've reached Thinking Plague status I think.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>Seabuckthorn – A Path Within A Path</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://laaps.bandcamp.com/album/a-path-within-a-path" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Do you want to listen for direction rather than following one? Put this in your headphones, then. Much like other <strong>Seabuckthorn</strong> records, the sounds arrive lightly, guitar, bowed strings, fragments of air and place, each leaving a trace before moving on. Nothing here insists on meaning, yet everything feels intentional. It’s music that trusts the listener to wander, to notice, and to find their own way through it.</em></span></li> </ul> 
<ul>
<li dir="auto">
<strong>tree // nature – Triple Heart Chamber and Dancing Mechanical Tree</strong>  
<a class="external-link" href="https://unknowntonerecords.bandcamp.com/album/triple-heart-chamber-and-dancing-mechanical-tree" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Listen/Purchase</a>
<br>
<em><span style="color: #808080;">Long drones stack, phase, and grind until repetition stops feeling passive and starts feeling structural, almost architectural. You can hear the mechanics working, oscillations nudging against each other, time stretching and folding in on itself. It’s visceral because of the volume and density, analytical because the process is always exposed. Music that doesn’t soothe so much as recalibrate your nervous system.</span></em></li> </ul>
