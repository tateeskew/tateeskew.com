Amy, my wife, got me the new [Oliver Sacks][1] book [Musicophilia][2] and it's really incredible. I've just started reading it, but the book grabs you at the preface. I've read one other sacks book, _Uncle Tungsten: Memories of a Chemical Boyhood_, which was really great. sacks is an amazing writer and quite a scientist (he's a neurologist). I think about how the brain reacts to music almost daily. I've also often wondered why humans seem to have some primal instinct that draws them towards music/rhythms and the reason why some people have a greater ability to "just get it" and a "brain" for it. Oliver Sacks covers this in an amazing fashion. if you love both science and music, this thing is a must-read. it's quite inspiring.
