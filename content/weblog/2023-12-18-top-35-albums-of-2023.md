## First Ten Standouts

* Pale Blue Eyes – This House [listen/purchase][1]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Layered melodic synth pop that recalls German motorik, Ulrich Schnauss production, and The Engineers – Always Returning era recordings</em></span>
* Scivic Rivers – S/T : [listen/purchase][2]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Kids don't write albums like this. Mature finely crafted songs in the vein of Talk Talk's later output and passing moments of Bob Welch Fleetwood Mac material</span>_
* Jeannine Schulz – Pure : [listen/purchase][3]  
_<span style="color: #808080;">Incredibly patient ambient micro-shifts of drift that feels completely organic</span>_
* Lankum – False Lankum [listen/purchase][4]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Irish folk </em></span><span style="color: #808080;"><i>ensemble bringing drones and minimalism to out of this world melodies</i></span>
* Simon Joyner & The Echoes – One Carried A Lantern : [listen/purchase][5]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>What else can be said about Simon? Inspired, practiced, and full of life lived songwriting</em></span>
* Blood Incantation – Luminescent Bridge : [listen/purchase][6]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Quite possibly the best death metal band going right now. Heavy, spacey, with ambient </em></span><span style="color: #808080;"><i>interludes</i></span>
* PJ Harvey – I Inside the Old Year Dying : [listen/purchase][7]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>PJ and John Parish continue to do extraordinary things together. This album is maybe her darkest since Is This Desire?</em></span>
* Läuten der Seele – Ertrunken Im Seichtesten Gewässer : [listen/purchase][8]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>If Christian releases an album every year…here it will be. Field recordings interspersed with very smart imaginative collage work</em></span>
* Ned Milligan – Considerable : [listen/purchase][9]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Quiet and delicate sounds that not only can get lost in the background of life, but overwhelm you with their intricacy when focused upon</em></span>
* Alberto Lizarralde – Haizetxe : [listen/purchase][10]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Collection of unreleased pieces from the maestro himself recorded in Basque on a 4 track reel to reel</em></span>

—

* James Ellis Ford – The Hum : [listen/purchase][11]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Think in the arena of Eno producing Brendan Perry and Barrett-era Floyd backing. Beautiful stuff</em></span>
* Scott Solter & Rohner Segnitz – The Murals : [listen/purchase][12]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Meticulously crafted dark ambient pieces that push and pull you with glistening ease. Stunning sound design</em></span>
* Ibex Clone – All Channels Clear : [listen/purchase][13]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Here is your Big Star ran through the LSD laced post punk of the Meat Puppets with a Bob Mould delivery</em></span>
* Tommy Guerrero – Amber of Memory : [listen/purchase][14]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Baritone guitars gently swaying in the wind drenched in sunny layers</em></span>
* A Journey of Giraffes – Empress Nouveau  : [listen/purchase][15]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Precise ideas of calm and reflection smartly captured to make you dream. I still don't believe John made this music in Baltimore</em></span>
* Vor-stellen – Parallelograms : [listen/purchase][16]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Improvised Krautrocky goodness served on a "band in a room" platter</em></span>
* Gary Peters – Beginnings: Collected Pedal Steel Works : [listen/purchase][17]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Gary situates you on a pastoral landscape, makes you dizzy from dissonance, and rocks you to sleep in the top of a tree with his pedal steel with this collection</em></span>
* John Francis Flynn – Look Over the Wall, See the Sky : [listen/purchase][18]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><span style="color: #808080;">Re-imagining traditional music with wit and high levels of creativity and passion</span></em></span>
* American Analog Set – For Forever : [listen/purchase][19]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Dropping a lot of the preciousness found in their music from decades ago, but keeping the minimalist approach to melody. Make dinner to this album…it'll taste better</em></span>
* Ori Barel – Alkaline River : [listen/purchase][20]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Modular synth compositions that are playful and push a sci-fi narrative. This isn't someone just fucking around with modular, this is well crafted stuff</em></span>
* Susan Alcorn Septeto del Sur – Canto : [listen/purchase][21]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Susan continues to be one of the best pedal steel players around. Rearrange your noggin' with a journey through improvised pieces that only masters at their craft can pull off</em></span>
* Tomáš Knoflí?ek – Serendipity : [listen/purchase][22]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Field recording deconstruction through electronic manipulation that challenges you upon each listen</em></span>
* Barn – Luminescence : [listen/purchase][23]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Brutal technical death metal that builds on what Chuck and his band Death </em></span><span style="color: #808080;"><i>originated. Watch out for these young guys. SO GOOD</i></span>
* Arthur Russell – Picture of Bunny Rabbit : [listen/purchase][24]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Unreleased recordings from the master. Wobbly tunes that exhibit what I love about personal recordings that you suspect will never see the light of day. </em></span>
* The Pines of Rome – The Unstruck Bell : [listen/purchase][25]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Look, we don't get many records like this anymore. Thanks to The Pines of Rome for making music again so that we can indulge in our slowcore daze again.</em></span>
* Cate Brooks – Tapeworks : [listen/purchase][26]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Perfectly arranged long form ambient passages that make me jealous that I didn't compose</em></span>
* Bill Orcutt – The Anxiety of Symmetry : [listen/purchase][27]  
<span style="color: #999999;"><em>Not your normal Orcutt guitar recording here. This delivers a layered loop approach that morphs as the repetitive fight of rhythmic dance ensues. Masterful in the vein of Philip Glass</em></span>
* Slowdive – everything is alive : [listen/purchase][28]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>You know what Slowdive sounds like, right? Well, this might possibly be the best album of their long career. After "Kisses" plays on the album, I expect Casey Kasem to come on and announce the next track.</em></span>
* The Necks – Travel : [listen/purchase][29]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em><span style="color: #808080;">Trio music brilliantly played and always rides the line of complex and completely accessible. In my opinion, their best since 2013's "Open"</span></em></span>
* Abstract Concrete – S/T : [listen/purchase][30]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>It seems everything Charles Heyward does, it turns to a delicious plate served up exactly how I like it. Experimental, smart, abstract post-everything chamber pop</em></span>
* Benoît Pioulard – Eidetic : [listen/purchase][31]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Thomas pours his influences all over every recording whether he's channeling masters of ambient, early-era Floyd, or Amon Düül II. When I hear this newest recording, I think Beck meant to make this record when he made Morning Phase</em></span>
* KMRU – glim : [listen/purchase][32]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Absolutely beautiful pacing on this mix of drone and micro-music as KMRU continues to grow into a fascinating artist</em></span>
* Justin Walter – Destroyer : [listen/purchase][33]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Imagine if you kept wanting a new Broadcast album that had 3-2-1 Contact segment transitional music. Justin made it for you right here</em></span>
* Giovanni Di Domenico – Succo di formiche : [listen/purchase][34]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Stunningly beautiful experiments in contemporary classical composition that makes you want to crawl into the recording studio</em></span>
* Yo La Tengo – This Stupid World : [listen/purchase][35]  
<span style="color: #808080;"><em>Still going strong after all of these years. When I hear "Aselestine" I'm instantly transported to a million cold Midwestern days stumbling to find out where I was going </em></span>
